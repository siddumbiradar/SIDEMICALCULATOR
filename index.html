About Loan EMI Calculator

A Loan EMI Calculator helps borrowers estimate their monthly loan payments quickly. EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount paid every month to repay a loan. It includes both the principal amount and the interest charged by the lender.

How EMI is Calculated

EMI is calculated using a standard mathematical formula based on the loan amount, interest rate, and loan tenure. The calculator automatically determines how much principal and interest you will pay every month.

What is Principal Amount?

The principal is the original loan amount borrowed from the lender. For example, if you take a loan of ₹10,00,000, the principal amount is ₹10,00,000.

What is Interest?

Interest is the cost of borrowing money from a bank or financial institution. It is calculated as a percentage of the principal amount and is paid along with the EMI.

What is Loan Prepayment?

Prepayment means paying a portion of the loan before the scheduled due date. Making prepayments can reduce the total interest paid and shorten the loan tenure.

Benefits of Using an EMI Calculator

  • Helps plan your loan repayment easily
  • Shows total interest payable
  • Allows comparison of loan tenures
  • Helps understand the impact of prepayments

About Recurring Deposit (RD)

A Recurring Deposit (RD) is a popular savings scheme offered by banks and financial institutions where you deposit a fixed amount every month for a fixed tenure and earn interest on it. It is ideal for individuals who want to build savings gradually with guaranteed returns.

How RD Interest is Calculated

In a recurring deposit, interest is calculated based on the deposit amount, interest rate, tenure, and compounding frequency. Each monthly deposit earns interest for the remaining tenure until maturity.

Generally, RD maturity value is calculated using the formula:

M = R × [(1 + i)^n − 1] / [1 − (1 + i)^(-1/3)]

  • M = Maturity value
  • R = Monthly deposit
  • i = Interest rate
  • n = Number of months

Benefits of Recurring Deposit

  • ✔ Safe investment with guaranteed returns
  • ✔ Encourages regular savings habit
  • ✔ Flexible tenure options
  • ✔ Higher interest than regular savings accounts
  • ✔ Suitable for short and medium term financial goals

Example

If you deposit ₹5,000 every month for 5 years at an interest rate of 7% per annum, the total investment will be ₹3,00,000 and the maturity value will be higher depending on the compounding frequency.

What is SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined investment method where an investor invests a fixed amount of money at regular intervals, usually monthly, into mutual funds or other investment instruments. SIP helps investors build wealth gradually through the power of compounding and rupee cost averaging.

Instead of investing a large lump sum amount at once, SIP allows investors to invest small amounts regularly. Over time, these investments grow and generate returns based on the expected rate of return.

How SIP is Calculated

SIP returns are calculated using the future value of a series of monthly investments compounded at a specific rate of return. The formula used to estimate SIP maturity value is:

Future Value of SIP = P × [((1 + r)^n − 1) / r] × (1 + r)

  • P = Monthly investment amount
  • r = Monthly expected return (annual return ÷ 12)
  • n = Total number of months invested

This calculator automatically applies this formula and shows the estimated future value of your investment along with total invested amount and total returns.

What is Step-Up SIP?

A Step-Up SIP means increasing your SIP investment amount periodically (yearly, half-yearly, or quarterly). As your income increases over time, you can increase your SIP contribution to accelerate wealth creation.

For example, if you start with a monthly SIP of ₹5,000 and increase it by 10% every year, your SIP amount becomes ₹5,500 next year, ₹6,050 the following year, and so on.

Importance of Step-Up SIP

  • Helps match investment with increasing income
  • Accelerates wealth creation
  • Helps achieve financial goals faster
  • Increases final investment value significantly

Even a small yearly increase in SIP amount can create a much larger investment corpus due to compounding.
